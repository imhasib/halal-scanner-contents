Privacy Policy — Halal Scanner

Last updated: 2026-05-01

This Privacy Policy describes how the Halal Scanner Android app (com.karigor.halalscanner, “the app”, “we”) handles information when you use it. The app is published by Karigor.

Summary
  • The app does not require an account and does not collect personally identifying information.
  • The camera is used only to read product barcodes on-device. Images are never saved or uploaded.
  • Barcodes you scan are sent to a public product database (Open Food Facts) to look up product information. No user identifier is attached.
  • Crash reports (no personal data, no scanned content) may be sent to Firebase Crashlytics to help us fix bugs.
  • Your scan history and settings stay on your device.

What the app collects and why

1. Camera — barcode reading only

When you open the scanner, the app uses your device camera to detect product barcodes via Google ML Kit on-device. The camera preview is processed in memory to extract the barcode value. No photos or video are stored on your device or transmitted off it. You can revoke the Camera permission at any time in Android settings; the rest of the app continues to work for browsing history and rulesets.

2. Network requests

The app makes outbound network calls to the following services:

  • Open Food Facts (https://world.openfoodfacts.org) — when you scan a barcode, the barcode is sent in a public API request to retrieve product data such as name, brand, ingredients, labels, allergens, and traces. Open Food Facts is an independent, publicly editable food product database. No user identifier or account information is included; the request carries only a User-Agent header containing the app’s name and version. See their terms of use for their policy.
  • Ruleset distribution (https://imhasib.github.io/halal-scanner-contents/) — the app periodically downloads the latest halal/haram/doubtful classification ruleset from this static endpoint. The request contains no user-identifying data beyond standard HTTP metadata (IP address, user agent) handled by the hosting provider (GitHub Pages).

The app does not operate its own backend, does not have user accounts, and does not transmit your scan history, location, contacts, device identifiers, or any other personal data to any server.

3. Crash reporting (Firebase Crashlytics)

If crash reporting is enabled in this build, unexpected crashes are reported to Firebase Crashlytics so we can diagnose and fix them. Crash reports include stack traces, the app version, the Android version, and the device model. They do not include scanned barcodes, product names, ingredients, your scan history, or any custom user data we deliberately attach — the app does not call Crashlytics’ user-id or custom-key APIs.

Firebase Crashlytics is a Google service; its handling of crash data is covered by Google’s privacy policy.

4. Data stored on your device

The app stores the following only on your device, in app-private storage:

  • Product cache — recently looked-up product data, kept briefly to avoid refetching (cache entries expire after ~24 hours).
  • Scan history — timestamp, barcode, product name, halal verdict, and flagged ingredients for each scan, so you can review past scans.
  • Ruleset data — the downloaded classification ruleset.
  • Settings — your in-app preferences (e.g. conservative mode, haptic and sound toggles).

This data is excluded from Android cloud backup and device-to-device transfer. It is removed when you uninstall the app or clear its storage from Android settings.

What the app does NOT do

  • No user accounts or sign-in.
  • No advertising, no ad SDKs, no ad identifiers.
  • No location tracking.
  • No contacts, microphone, SMS, call log, or file-system browsing access.
  • No selling or sharing of personal information with third parties for advertising or marketing.
